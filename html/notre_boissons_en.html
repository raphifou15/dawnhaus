Drink dawnhaus

discover dawnhaus

We offer you a drink with a unique taste and just enough sweetness, the result of a year of research and tests to achieve the ultimate taste. Like a nose for a perfume, we have improved the Dawnhaus taste experience day after day to provide the ultimate taste and pleasure experience for every moment of your life.

Since ancient times, the hibiscus flower, known for its antioxidant power and soothing effect, has been used by communities in South America, the Caribbean and Africa to make a delicious, refreshing and unifying drink.

Dawnhaus has drawn on this ancestral gift to create a new kind of drink, a 2.0 drink that represents modernity and the blending of cultures without forgetting what it owes to our common past.

As a drink of the 21st century, Dawnhaus adapts to the new demands of the people: Antioxidant, based on organic hibiscus flowers, with half as much sugar as the world's most consumed soft drink.

ingredients used

Hibiscus

Since ancestral times, the hibiscus flower, known for its antioxidant power and appeasing effect has been used by communities in South America, the Caribbean and Africa to make a delicious refreshing and gathering drink.

Dawnhaus drew on this ancestral gift to create a drink of a new kind, a 2.0 drink that represent modernity and the mix of cultures without forgetting what it owes to our common past.

As a 21st century drink, Danhaus fits with people’s new requirements: Antioxidant, made with organic hibiscus flowers, with Half the amount of sugar compared to the most consumed soft drink worldwide

Sugar

When creating a drink, there are 3 important variables that will influence the taste and value of the drink: The strength of the bubbles - the brewing time - the sugar.

Dawnhaus has made the choice of harmony between these 3 elements. On the one hand, an infusion just perfect to obtain a good taste and a beautiful color. On the other hand, intense and light bubbles, and above all... a controlled amount of sugar! Dawnhaus contains 7.2 grams of sugar per 100ml, just enough to give an intense and sweet taste. This corresponds to more than 30% less sugar than the most popular soft drinks, in which you can easily find more than 10g per 100ml.
